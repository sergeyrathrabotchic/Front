The person I admire most of all is Lady Diana Spenser, a former member of the British royal family. Diana Francis Spencer, Her Highness the Princess of Wales, was born on 1 July 1961 in Norfolk County to an English aristocratic family. She was called the princess of hearts, the people's princess, and a fashion icon for all time. The future princess's biography was perfect. Diana received her primary education at Sandringham. Later the girl attended Sylfield Private School and after that studied at Riddlesworth Hall. As a child the character of the future crown princess was not difficult, but she was always determined even stubborn sometimes. Diana studied briefly in Switzerland, but returned home due to intense homesickness. After finishing her studies, she started working as a nanny and kindergarten teacher in the prestigious Knightsbridge area of London. Princess Diana was much loved by the people of Britain, who affectionately called her Lady Di. Princess Diana was a philanthropist, donating money to various foundations, an activist in the movement to ban land mines, providing material and moral help to people. Princess Diana cared for 100 organizations which she regularly visited. Moreover, Lady Di impressed British citizens with her bravery and sincere involvement in the problems of hospitals, orphan asylums and homes for the elderly. On 31 August 1997, Diana was killed in a car crash during her visit to Paris. Frankly speaking, I admire her because her activism inspired many people, her image and style made her an international icon. To my mind, by looking at her photos you can tell how creative and imaginative she was. When I think of her, I am sure that we should be kinder and more tolerant to each other.
